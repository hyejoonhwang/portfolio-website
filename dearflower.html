Overview

Create and share digital bouquets with friends

Dear Flower lets people arrange their own digital bouquet — choosing from different flower types and wrapper styles — then pair it with a handwritten letter and send it to someone through a shareable link. On the receiving end, the bouquet is revealed through an envelope-opening moment that makes the experience feel personal and intentional.

I designed and built the entire experience end-to-end, from the drag-and-drop canvas interaction to the sharing flow and the recipient reveal. The focus was on creating something that felt craft-like and tangible in a digital space — where the act of making the bouquet is just as meaningful as receiving one.

How It Works

The experience is structured around four moments

The flow is designed around two distinct roles — the creator and the recipient — with a deliberate handoff in between. The creator side is active and expressive: arranging, writing, composing something personal. The recipient side is about anticipation and reveal. The envelope-opening moment was an intentional design choice to create a pause before the gift appears, making the experience feel more like unwrapping something physical than just loading a page.
