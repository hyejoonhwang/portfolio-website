Touch of Moss

An interactive installation where touching moss triggers pulsing LEDs and ambient sound — a metaphor for human connection with nature's rhythm.

Role

Design & Fabrication

The Team

Summer & Alua

Context

ITP Physical Computing

Overview

What if nature could respond to your touch?

Touch of Moss is a moss-covered wall embedded with LED netting and handmade soft switches. When a visitor presses into the moss, the surrounding LEDs pulse in a heartbeat-like pattern and ambient forest sounds fill the space — as if the earth is responding to the presence of a human hand. The installation was designed to create a quiet, sensory moment where people slow down and feel a connection to something living.

I handled the physical design and fabrication — 3D-printing dome structures in Fusion 360 to give the moss blobs varying heights, building soft switches from yoga mats and copper plates, and wiring 400 NeoPixel LEDs across the panel. The interaction is intentionally simple: touch, and the wall breathes back. The heartbeat animation was a deliberate choice to make the response feel organic rather than mechanical, reinforcing the idea that nature has its own pulse.
